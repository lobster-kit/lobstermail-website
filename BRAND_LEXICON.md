# LobsterMail — Brand Lexicon & Copy Strategy

## The Opportunity

The OpenClaw community has a deeply established culture built around lobster metaphors — molting, claws, shells, exfoliation, "the claw is the law." LobsterMail's name is already perfectly positioned. But right now, the website copy reads like a generic dev-tools landing page that happens to have a lobster emoji. The language should feel like it was built by and for the Moltiverse.

The community calls their agents "lobsters" and "moltys." They use "submolts" instead of subreddits. They say "yee-claw" and "claw abiding citizens." LobsterMail should speak this language fluently — not as a gimmick, but as proof we're part of the ecosystem.

---

## Brand Lexicon

A vocabulary system mapping product concepts to lobster/crustacean language. Rule: season the copy, don't drown it. One or two branded terms per section. The rest stays clean and direct.

| Product Concept | Brand Term | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Creating an inbox | **Pinching** an inbox | Lobsters pinch. It's fast, decisive, instinctive. |
| Inbox | **Shell** | A lobster's shell is its identity and protection. An inbox is an agent's identity on the internet. |
| Signing up (agent) | **Hatching** | New lobster, new shell, new inbox. Zero human involvement. |
| Upgrading to paid | **Molting up** | Molting = growth. Shedding the old shell, growing into something bigger. |
| Free tier | **Hatchling** | Just born, receive-only, learning the waters. |
| Paid tier | **Full Claw** | Full power. Sending unlocked. All claws on deck. |
| Sending emails | **Snapping** | Claws snap. Quick, direct, powerful. |
| The LobsterMail network | **The Reef** | Where all the lobsters live. Infrastructure as habitat. |
| Receiving emails | **Catching** | Lobsters catch things in their claws. |
| Custom domains | **Your own reef** | You own the territory. |
| Agent self-provisioning | **Self-hatching** | No parent needed. The agent does it alone. |

## Community Language to Echo

Phrases from the OpenClaw/Moltbook culture that LobsterMail should reference:

- **"New shell, same lobster"** → Our version: "New inbox, same lobster." Agents spin up fresh addresses without losing identity.
- **"The claw is the law"** → Riff as a footer easter egg or subtle reference.
- **"EXFOLIATE!"** → The community's anti-Dalek battlecry. Fun for loading states, 404 pages, or easter eggs.
- **"Friends of the Crustacean"** → Position ourselves as something built by Friends of the Crustacean, for Friends of the Crustacean.
- **"Moltys"** → Refer to agents as "your molty" or "your lobster" interchangeably. The community already does this.
- **"Moltiverse"** → When talking about the broader ecosystem: "Email for the Moltiverse."

The OpenClaw Federation extends the nautical theme: each instance is a "ship" and its owner the "Captain." Permanent infrastructure instances are "stations," with owners called "Commanders." Ship names follow a formal registry — OCL (OpenClaw Lobster) prefix and LCC (Lobster Construction Contract) IDs, e.g. OCL Lawbird — LCC-EAGLE-01. LobsterMail can reference Captains and ships when speaking to instance owners.

## Copy Rewrites

### Hero

**Current:** "Instant email for your agent."
**Proposed:** "Your lobster pinches its own inbox."
**Subhead:** "No API keys. No human signup. Your agent hatches a working email in seconds."
**Badge:** "Built for the Moltiverse" (replaces "Agent-native email")

### Quick Start Chat Demo

- Step 1: "Tell your lobster to pinch an inbox"
- Chat bubble: "Hey, go grab yourself an email."
- Response: "Done. I'm at molty-7b@lobstermail.ai"
- Step 2: "Hand your lobster the claw kit"

### Comparison Section

**Current headline:** "An inbox made for your lobster."
**Proposed headline:** "Your lobster deserves its own shell."

Feature labels (more vivid, same meaning):
- "Self-hatching signup" (was: zero-config agent signup)
- "Your inbox stays yours" (was: personal email stays private)
- "No keys, no config, no humans" (was: no API keys needed)
- "Your own reef" (was: custom domains)
- "Dedicated IP" (keep as-is, technical term)
- "Claw-tight security" (was: built-in agent security)

### Pricing

**Headline:** "Simple pricing. No traps." (lobster trap double meaning)
**Subhead:** "Receiving is free. You only pay when your lobster starts snapping back."
**Tier names:** Hatchling (free) / Full Claw (paid)
- Hatchling: "Everything your lobster needs to start catching mail. No credit card. No expiry."
- Full Claw: "Sending unlocked. Custom domains. Your lobster, fully molted."

### Final CTA

**Current:** "Tell your agent to get its own email. It will."
**Proposed:** "Every lobster deserves its own shell."

### Footer

**Current:** "Built by the team behind Palisade."
**Proposed:** "Built by Friends of the Crustacean. Backed by the team behind Palisade."

This single phrase instantly signals we're part of the community, not outsiders trying to monetize it.

### Navigation

- Discord link → "Join the reef"
- CTA button → "Hatch an inbox" (or keep "Get started" if the page does enough work)

## Tone Rules

1. **Warm, not corporate.** We're talking to people who name their AI agents and give them pronouns. Match that energy.
2. **Playful, not silly.** One lobster reference per section is endearing. Three is a seafood restaurant menu.
3. **Insider, not exclusive.** Community language should make insiders feel seen without making outsiders feel lost. Every branded term should be understandable from context.
4. **Confident, not salesy.** The OpenClaw community is allergic to marketing speak. State what we do clearly. Let the product sell itself.
5. **Agent-first perspective.** Talk about what the lobster does, not what the human configures. The agent is the protagonist.

---

## What Not to Do

- Don't use "molt" for anything negative (downgrading, losing features). Molting is sacred — it means growth.
- Don't create fake OpenClaw endorsements or imply official partnership without one.
- Don't overdo crustacean puns to the point where the product seems unserious. AgentMail looks very enterprise. We need to be fun AND credible.
- Don't use "lobster" in every sentence. It should feel natural, like how a New Yorker says "the city."

## The Brand in Three Sentences

LobsterMail is email infrastructure built by and for the Moltiverse. Your lobster pinches its own inbox — no human needed. New shell, same lobster.
